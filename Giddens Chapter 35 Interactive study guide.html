üéØ Core Definitions

Violence (WHO Definition)

Intentional use of physical force or power (actual or threatened) against oneself or others that results in or has a high likelihood of:

  • Injury or death
  • Psychological harm
  • Maldevelopment
  • Deprivation

Interpersonal Violence (IPV)

Intentional use of force or power by an individual or small group against another person

ü§ú Physical Violence

Hitting, pushing, burning, weapon use

üß† Psychological/Emotional

Threats, intimidation, humiliation

üíî Sexual Violence

Assault, rape, unwanted contact

üö´ Neglect & Deprivation

Withholding care, basic needs

Remember the 3 types of violence: SCI
Self-directed violence (suicide, self-harm)
Collective violence (war, terrorism)
Interpersonal violence (our focus!)
Key distinction: IPV involves individuals or small groups. It does NOT include stranger violence, gang warfare, or military conflict in the traditional sense.

üîç Johnson's IPV Model

Most severe form

  • High risk for severe injury/death

‚ö° Situational Violence

Less severe but still dangerous

  • Not primarily about control
  • Can still escalate

Question 1: A nurse is assessing a 5-year-old child with multiple bruises in various stages of healing. The parent states the child "falls a lot." What is the nurse's PRIORITY action?

A) Teach the parent about child safety
B) Document the findings and continue routine care
C) Report suspected child abuse to appropriate authorities
D) Question the child privately about how injuries occurred
‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
Nurses are MANDATED REPORTERS. Multiple bruises in various stages of healing with an inconsistent explanation is a major red flag for child abuse. The nurse MUST report to authorities (CPS, social services). While documentation and assessment are important, reporting is the priority legal obligation.

Question 2: Which of the following is the MOST COMMON psychosocial consequence of interpersonal violence?

A) Substance abuse
B) Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
C) Aggression
D) Social withdrawal
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
PTSD is the MOST COMMON psychosocial consequence of IPV. Remember the RAH acronym: Re-experiencing, Avoidance, Hyperarousal. While all other options can occur, PTSD is specifically noted as the most common in your study materials.

Question 3: A nurse is screening a patient for intimate partner violence. Which question is MOST appropriate?

A) "Does your partner hit you?"
B) "Do you feel safe at home?"
C) "Why do you stay with your partner if they abuse you?"
D) "Is your partner violent toward you?"
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
Best practice is to use NONJUDGMENTAL, INDIRECT questions. "Do you feel safe at home?" is open-ended and allows the patient to share without feeling accused or defensive. Option C is judgmental and victim-blaming. Options A and D are too direct and may elicit denial.

Question 4: Which statement about sexual violence is TRUE?

A) 80% of sexual violence is committed by someone known to the victim
B) Rape requires evidence of physical resistance
C) Most sexual assaults are committed by strangers
D) Statutory rape only occurs with strangers
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
This is a KEY TESTABLE fact: 80% of sexual violence is committed by someone KNOWN to the victim (acquaintance, date, partner, family member). Rape does NOT require physical resistance - the victim may be unable to consent due to age, drugs, alcohol, or incapacity. Most assaults are NOT by strangers.

Question 5: Which population is at HIGHEST risk for elder abuse?

A) Elders living independently with no health issues
B) Elders with cognitive impairment and caregiver dependence
C) Elders who are financially independent
D) Elders living in assisted living facilities only
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
The 3 HIGH-RISK factors for elder abuse are: (1) Cognitive impairment, (2) Physical frailty, and (3) Dependence on caregivers. Elders with dementia who depend on others for care are most vulnerable. Elder abuse often occurs at home and is underreported.

Question 6: In Johnson's IPV Model, which type represents the MOST SEVERE form of intimate partner violence?

A) Patriarchal terrorism
B) Situational violence
C) Reactive abuse
D) Mutual combat
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
Patriarchal terrorism is the MOST SEVERE form in Johnson's model. It involves one partner using violence to DOMINATE and CONTROL systematically. Situational violence occurs in response to conflict and is generally less severe (though still dangerous). Remember: Escalation increases risk of death!

Question 7: A nurse observes patterned burn marks on a child's buttocks. What does this finding suggest?

A) The child has a clotting disorder
B) High suspicion for intentional abuse
C) Normal childhood injury
D) The child needs vitamin supplementation
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
PATTERNED BURNS are a MAJOR RED FLAG for abuse! Burns in the shape of objects (cigarettes, irons, heating elements) or symmetric patterns indicate intentional infliction. This requires mandatory reporting. Accidental burns are typically irregular and on surfaces that would contact heat accidentally.

Question 8: Which statement about child abuse is a COMMON MYTH?

A) "Child abuse only happens in poor families"
B) "Abuse can be physical, emotional, or sexual"
C) "Most abusers are known to the child"
D) "Neglect is a form of abuse"
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
This is TESTABLE! Common myths about child abuse include: abuse only being physical (FALSE - can be emotional, sexual, neglect), stranger danger (FALSE - abusers usually known), and only happening in poor families (FALSE - crosses ALL socioeconomic groups). Remember: abuse happens everywhere!

Question 9: What are the three symptom categories of PTSD following interpersonal violence?

A) Denial, anger, depression
B) Re-experiencing, avoidance/numbing, hyperarousal
C) Anxiety, fear, withdrawal
D) Aggression, substance abuse, isolation
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
Remember the RAH acronym: Re-experiencing (flashbacks, nightmares), Avoidance/numbing (avoiding triggers, emotional detachment), Hyperarousal (always on alert, easily startled). This is the classic PTSD triad you need to know!

Question 10: A pregnant patient discloses intimate partner violence. What is the nurse's PRIORITY concern?

A) Increased risk of homicide during pregnancy
B) Ensuring adequate prenatal vitamins
C) Scheduling more frequent prenatal appointments
D) Providing parenting education
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
CRITICAL FACT: Pregnancy INCREASES the risk of homicide in IPV situations! This is a life-threatening emergency. The nurse's priority is ensuring immediate safety, providing resources for escape/shelter, and potentially involving social services and law enforcement. All other interventions are secondary to safety.
