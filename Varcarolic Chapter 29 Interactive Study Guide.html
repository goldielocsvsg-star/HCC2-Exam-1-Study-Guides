Question 1: What is the MOST COMMON date-rape drug?

A) Alcohol
B) Rohypnol
C) GHB
D) Ketamine
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
ALCOHOL is the most common date-rape drug! While GHB, Rohypnol, and Ketamine are all used, alcohol remains the #1 substance used to facilitate sexual assault because it's legal, readily available, and socially acceptable.

Question 2: A 20-year-old patient presents to the ED after a suspected sexual assault. What is the nurse's PRIORITY action?

A) Obtain a detailed sexual history
B) Ensure privacy and safety
C) Call the police immediately
D) Begin the forensic examination
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
The PRIORITY is ensuring privacy and safety first. Create a safe, private environment before proceeding with assessment or evidence collection. The patient should feel secure and protected. Police notification is the patient's choice (unless mandated for minors/dependent adults).

Question 3: Which statement about sexual assault perpetrators is TRUE?

A) Most perpetrators are known to the victim
B) Most assaults are committed by strangers
C) Stranger rape is more common than acquaintance rape
D) Sexual assault rarely involves intimate partners
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
This is VERY TESTABLE! Most perpetrators are KNOWN to the victim - they may be intimate partners, acquaintances, or dates. Stranger rape is actually LESS COMMON than commonly believed. Nearly half of female victims are raped by acquaintances.

Question 4: What percentage of first rapes occur before age 25?

A) 40%
B) 50%
C) 65%
D) 79%
‚úÖ Correct Answer: D
TESTABLE NUMBER: 79% of first rapes occur BEFORE age 25, and approximately 40% occur before age 18. This highlights the vulnerability of young people to sexual assault.

Question 5: A nurse is caring for a sexual assault survivor who states, "I should have fought harder. This is my fault." What is the BEST response?

A) "Why didn't you fight back?"
B) "You need to stop blaming yourself"
C) "This was not your fault. You survived, and that's what matters."
D) "Let's focus on the evidence collection"
‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
The MOST IMPORTANT message is "This was NOT your fault." Validate survival, normalize trauma responses (freezing is common), and provide emotional support. Never ask "why" questions (option A) as they can sound blaming. Avoid dismissive statements (option B). While evidence is important, emotional support is the priority when patient expresses self-blame.

Question 6: Which condition indicates a patient CANNOT legally give consent for sexual activity?

A) Being in a committed relationship
B) Being intoxicated
C) Having previous sexual experience
D) Being physically attracted to someone
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
Consent CANNOT be given when intoxicated or drugged! Remember MITU-T: Minor, Intoxicated, Threatened, Unconscious, Thinking impaired. All of these conditions prevent legal consent.

Question 7: A patient has no visible physical injuries after reporting sexual assault. What should the nurse understand?

A) The assault probably didn't happen
B) The patient is likely lying
C) Absence of injury does not mean assault didn't occur
D) No evidence collection is needed
‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
CRITICAL CONCEPT: Lack of physical injury ‚â† lack of assault! Many sexual assault victims have NO visible injuries. The nurse should BELIEVE the patient and proceed with appropriate care and evidence collection. Never question the validity of the assault based on absence of injuries.

Question 8: Which statement about the Violence Against Women Act (VAWA) is correct?

A) Victims must pay for their own sexual assault exams
B) States must pay for sexual assault exams; victims not required to report to police
C) Victims must file a police report to receive an exam
D) Only federal facilities must provide free exams
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
VAWA (1994) mandates that states MUST pay for sexual assault exams and victims are NOT required to report to police to receive care. This removes financial barriers and respects patient autonomy. No cost to victim, regardless of whether they choose to involve law enforcement.

Question 9: What is the primary focus of rape?

A) Sexual desire
B) Power and control
C) Physical attraction
D) Romantic feelings
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
MYTH vs FACT! Rape is about POWER and CONTROL, NOT sexual desire or attraction. This is a common myth that's frequently tested. Understanding this helps nurses provide better trauma-informed care and education.

Question 10: A nurse is collecting evidence from a sexual assault victim. How should clothing be stored?

A) In plastic bags to preserve moisture
B) In paper bags to prevent mold and degradation
C) In the patient's personal belongings
D) Clothing should be discarded, not collected
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
FORENSIC NURSING: Always use PAPER bags for clothing evidence, NEVER plastic! Plastic bags trap moisture and can cause mold/degradation of biological evidence (DNA, fluids). Paper bags allow evidence to breathe and preserve it properly. This maintains chain of custody and evidence integrity.

Question 11: Which patient characteristic indicates HIGH RISK for suicide after sexual assault?

A) Having strong family support
B) History of assault plus self-blame
C) Engaging with counseling services
D) Expressing anger at the perpetrator
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
CRITICAL: History of assault + self-blame = HIGH SUICIDE RISK! This is a priority assessment finding. Self-blame increases risk significantly. The nurse must assess for suicidal ideation and implement safety measures. This is one of the six priority assessment areas (ACES + PT).

Question 12: What is the legal definition of statutory rape?

A) Rape by a stranger in a public place
B) Rape involving physical force
C) Sexual activity with a minor who cannot legally consent
D) Rape that occurs within marriage
‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
Statutory rape = sexual activity with a MINOR who cannot legally consent. Age of consent is 16-18 (varies by state). Even if the minor "agrees," it's still rape because they cannot legally consent due to age. This is different from other forms of rape.
