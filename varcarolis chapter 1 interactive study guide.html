Mental Health (WHO Definition)

A state of well-being in which individuals:

  • Realize their abilities
  • Cope with normal stresses of life
  • Work productively
  • Contribute to their community
üîç

Mental Illness

Clinically diagnosable psychiatric disorders causing:

  • Disturbances in thinking
  • Changes in mood
  • Alterations in behavior
  • Impairments in functioning

üí° Remember This!

There is no physical health without mental health. They are inseparably linked!

üõ°Ô∏è Risk & Protective Factors

+

Risk Factors increase likelihood of mental illness:

  • Genetic vulnerability
  • Brain chemistry imbalance
  • Prenatal exposure (alcohol, oxygen deprivation)
  • Trauma, chronic stress, poverty
  • Lack of social support

Protective Factors reduce risk:

  • Strong relationships & social support
  • Stable housing & education
  • Access to healthcare
  • Coping skills & resilience

üí™ Resilience

+

Resilience is the ability to:

  • Adapt to adversity
  • Regulate emotions
  • Think positively
  • Solve problems
  • Seek help when needed

Key insight: Resilient people are NOT stress-free‚Äîthey simply recover faster and stronger!

üß¨ Nature vs. Nurture: The Diathesis-Stress Model

+

Mental illness develops from:

Biological Predisposition + Environmental Stress = Mental Illness

This explains why one person becomes ill under stress while another does not. Both genetics AND life experience shape brain chemistry and behavior.

üåç Cultural Influence on Mental Health

+

Culture shapes:

  • What is considered "normal"
  • How symptoms are expressed
  • Whether people seek treatment
  • How families respond to illness

Stigma: Shame and social rejection associated with mental illness

Culture-bound syndromes: Disorders occurring in specific cultures (e.g., "running amok" in Southeast Asia)

‚öñÔ∏è Public Policy & Mental Health Parity

+

Mental Health Parity Acts require insurance companies to treat mental illness equally to physical illness.

Affordable Care Act (2010) expanded coverage by:

  • Making mental health an essential benefit
  • Protecting preexisting conditions
  • Allowing young adults on parents' insurance until age 26

üìä Epidemiology Basics

+

Incidence: Number of NEW cases in a time period

Prevalence: TOTAL number of existing cases

Comorbidity: Presence of multiple disorders in one person

Purpose: Identify high-risk populations, plan prevention, improve healthcare systems.

üìã DSM-5 & Classification

+

The DSM-5 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual) is the primary diagnostic manual in the U.S.

Used for:

  • Diagnosing disorders
  • Planning treatment
  • Research
  • Insurance reimbursement

Important: DSM classifies DISORDERS, not people!

Key Terms

Master these essential vocabulary words

Mental Health

State of well-being where individuals realize abilities, cope with stress, work productively, and contribute to community.

Mental Illness

Psychiatric disorders with definable diagnoses causing dysfunctions in thinking, mood, or behavior.

Resilience

The ability and capacity to secure resources needed to support well-being; adapting positively to adversity.

Stigma

The belief that the overall person is flawed; characterized by social shunning, disgrace, and shame.

Diathesis-Stress Model

Theory that mental illness results from biological predisposition combined with environmental stress.

Recovery

A process of change through which individuals improve health, live self-directed lives, and reach full potential.

Incidence

Number of NEW cases of a disorder in a healthy population within a given time period.

Prevalence

TOTAL number of cases (new and existing) in a population during a specific time period.

Comorbidity

Presence of two or more disorders occurring at the same time or in sequence.

DSM-5

Diagnostic and Statistical Manual, Fifth Edition‚Äîprimary classification system for mental disorders in the U.S.

Cultural Competence

Ability to provide culturally sensitive care that considers patients' cultural backgrounds and beliefs.

Mental Health Continuum

Conceptual model showing mental health and illness as points along a spectrum rather than distinct categories.

PMH-RN

Psychiatric-Mental Health Registered Nurse‚Äîprovides basic psychiatric nursing care.

PMH-APRN

Psychiatric-Mental Health Advanced Practice Registered Nurse‚Äîcan diagnose and prescribe medications.

Epidemiology

Quantitative study of the distribution of mental disorders in human populations.
