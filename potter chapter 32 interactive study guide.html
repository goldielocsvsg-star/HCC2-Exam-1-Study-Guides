üßò Relaxation Therapy

+

Progressive Relaxation

Sequential tightening & releasing of muscle groups. Teaches awareness of tension vs relaxation.

Best for: Physically healthy patients with adequate energy

Passive Relaxation

Breathing + imagery WITHOUT muscle contraction. Still the mind and body intentionally.

Best for: Frail, severely ill, or low-energy patients

Clinical Effects of Relaxation:

  • ‚Üì Pain and anxiety
  • ‚Üì Blood pressure and muscle tension
  • ‚Üì Opioid use
  • ‚Üë Sense of control
  • ‚Üë Well-being and positive psychological state

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations

Progressive relaxation is inappropriate for patients with advanced disease or decreased energy reserves. Use passive relaxation or guided imagery for these individuals.

üßò‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Meditation & Breathing

+

Purpose: Develop MINDFULNESS, not just relaxation. Become more aware of self.

Five Elements of Meditation:

  • 1. Quiet environment
  • 2. Comfortable position
  • 3. Receptive attitude
  • 4. Focus of attention
  • 5. Relaxed breathing

Clinical Benefits:

  • Improves sleep quality
  • Reduces depression & anxiety
  • Enhances coping
  • Improves quality of life
  • Improves mood & sense of identity

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations

Not ideal for patients with fear of losing control. Some patients become hypertensive during meditation. May reduce need for antihypertensive, thyroid, or psychotropic medications‚Äîmonitor closely!

üñºÔ∏è Imagery & Creative Visualization

+

Imagery: Uses conscious mind to create mental images that stimulate physical changes, improve well-being, and enhance self-awareness.

Creative Visualization: Goal-directed imagery for personal growth & healing‚Äîvisualize a future that aids personal development.

Clinical Applications:

  • Pain control and management
  • Sleep improvement
  • Anxiety reduction
  • Chronic conditions: asthma, cancer, migraines
  • GI disorders (IBS, ulcerative colitis)
  • Autoimmune disorders
  • Menstrual syndromes

üí° Teaching Point

Individualize imagery scenarios for each patient. Ensure images don't evoke negative memories. Use ALL senses: visual, auditory, proprioceptive, gustatory, and olfactory.

üìç Acupuncture

+

Mechanism: Restores flow of QI through MERIDIANS via ACUPOINTS. Thin needles inserted to realign vital energy.

Effective For:

  • Low back pain & myofascial pain
  • Chemotherapy-induced nausea/vomiting
  • Tension headaches & migraine prevention
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Anxiety & depression
  • Hot flashes & menopausal symptoms
  • Fatigue
  • Postoperative pain

‚ö†Ô∏è Precautions & Contraindications

Use caution with: pregnant patients, history of seizures, hepatitis carriers, HIV-positive patients. Contraindicated: bleeding disorders, skin infections, valvular heart disease (semipermanent needles). Electroacupuncture contraindicated with pacemakers, arrhythmias, epilepsy, pregnancy.

üåø Natural Products & Herbal Therapy

+

‚ö†Ô∏è Major Safety Issue: DRUG INTERACTIONS

Herbal medicines are NOT regulated by FDA! Always ask patients about ALL herbs, teas, supplements. Many interact with conventional medications (anticoagulants, cardiac drugs, HIV medications). Some patients don't consider these as "medications."

Common Herb Interactions to Know:

  • Garlic ‚Äî Interacts with warfarin & blood thinners
  • Ginkgo ‚Äî Interacts with anticoagulants, NSAIDs
  • Ginger ‚Äî Interacts with warfarin, anticoagulants
  • Valerian ‚Äî Interacts with barbiturates, alcohol
  • Echinacea ‚Äî Interacts with immunosuppressants
  • Licorice ‚Äî Interacts with digoxin, antihypertensives

ü©∫ Nursing Clinical Judgment Framework

When recommending therapies, evaluate: (1) Evidence of benefit & risk, (2) Patient condition & energy level, (3) Cultural beliefs, (4) Patient preferences, (5) Interaction with medications, (6) Need for specialized referral

Key Terms

Master these essential CAM vocabulary words

Allopathic/Biomedicine

Conventional Western medicine focusing on disease, pathology, and pharmacologic/surgical treatment.

Complementary Therapy

Therapies used TOGETHER WITH conventional treatment recommended by health care provider.

Alternative Therapy

Therapies used INSTEAD OF conventional medical treatment; become primary treatment.

Integrative Therapy

Evidence-based combination of conventional and complementary therapies to achieve optimal health.

Integrative Health Care

System where patients are treated by a team using both biomedical and complementary approaches.

Integrative Nursing

Whole-person care grounded in evidence, using traditional and emerging interventions through caring relationships.

Whole Medical Systems

Complete systems of theory and practice that evolved independently from biomedicine (TCM, Ayurveda, etc.).

Traditional Chinese Medicine

Ancient healing system focused on balancing yin/yang energies; includes acupuncture, herbs, qi gong, tai chi.

Qi (Vital Energy)

Life force or vital energy in TCM that flows through meridians; imbalance causes disease.

Yin and Yang

Opposing yet complementary forces in TCM that must stay balanced for health (cold/hot, dark/light).

Acupoints

Specific points along meridians where acupuncture needles are inserted to influence qi flow.

Acupuncture

Inserting thin needles at acupoints along meridians to regulate qi and produce therapeutic effects.

Moxibustion

Burning moxa (dried herbs) on or near the skin to apply heat and promote healing.

Cupping

Placing heated cups on the skin to create slight suction for therapeutic effect.

Tai Chi

Moving meditation incorporating breath, movement, and meditation to balance qi; improves balance.

Qi Gong

Choreographed movements designed to promote and manipulate the flow of qi within the body.

Relaxation Response

Physiological state characterized by ‚ÜìHR, ‚ÜìBP, ‚ÜìO2 consumption, promoting healing and calm.

Progressive Relaxation

Sequentially tightening and releasing muscle groups to learn difference between tension and relaxation.

Passive Relaxation

Stilling mind and body through breathing and imagery WITHOUT muscle contraction; for low-energy patients.

Meditation

Activity that limits stimulus input by directing attention to single focus to develop mindfulness.

Imagery/Visualization

Using conscious mind to create mental images that stimulate physical changes and improve well-being.

Creative Visualization

Goal-directed imagery to visualize a future that aids personal development and desired change.

Biofeedback

Using instruments to provide feedback about physiological functions; teaches voluntary self-control.

Therapeutic Touch (TT)

Energy-based therapy using practitioner's hands on or near body to influence patient's energy field.

Pilates

Exercise system focusing on centering, control, precision, and breathing to improve strength and balance.
