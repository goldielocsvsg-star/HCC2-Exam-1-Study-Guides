Question 1: What is the MOST COMMON type of child abuse?

B) Sexual abuse

‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
NEGLECT is the most common type of child abuse, accounting for approximately 73% of cases! This is a highly testable statistic. Neglect involves failure to provide basic physical, emotional, educational, or medical needs.

Question 2: When is the MOST DANGEROUS time in an intimate partner violence relationship?

A) During the honeymoon phase
B) During the tension-building phase
C) When attempting to leave the relationship
D) During pregnancy
‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
The HIGHEST DANGER PERIOD is when attempting to LEAVE the relationship! This is when homicide risk is greatest. This is why safety planning is so critical - never ask "Why don't you just leave?" as it oversimplifies an extremely dangerous situation.

Question 3: What percentage of child abuse perpetrators are parents?

A) 50%
B) 70%
C) 92%
D) 99%
‚úÖ Correct Answer: C
TESTABLE NUMBER: Approximately 92% of child abuse perpetrators are PARENTS! This dispels the "stranger danger" myth. Most abuse happens within the family, which is why screening and intervention are so important.

Question 4: A nurse suspects child abuse. What is required before making a report?

A) Definitive proof of abuse
B) Reasonable suspicion
C) Confession from the perpetrator
D) Permission from the supervisor
‚úÖ Correct Answer: B
REASONABLE SUSPICION is enough! Nurses are mandated reporters - you do NOT need proof. If something seems "off," report it. The authorities will investigate. It's better to report and be wrong than to miss abuse.

Question 5: Which finding in an infant is HIGHLY suspicious for abuse?

B) Diaper rash
C) Cradle cap
D) Mongolian spots
‚úÖ Correct Answer: A
ANY bruising in infants <6 months is HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS for abuse! Babies this young are not mobile enough to injure themselves through normal activity. This is a major red flag requiring immediate investigation.

The HONEYMOON PHASE involves apologies, gifts, promises like "It will never happen again," and temporary calm. This is why victims often return - they believe the abuser will change. Remember TAH: Tension-building, Acute battering, Honeymoon. The cycle repeats and escalates without intervention.
