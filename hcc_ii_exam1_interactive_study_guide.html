Scenario 1: Suspected Abuse
You are admitting a 72-year-old woman to the medical unit. During your assessment, you notice multiple bruises in various stages of healing on her arms and torso. When you ask about the bruises, she becomes quiet and looks at her daughter who is in the room. The daughter quickly says, "Mom is so clumsy, she's always falling." You notice the patient seems fearful and won't make eye contact.
What is your PRIORITY nursing action?
A. Confront the daughter about the inconsistent story
B. Ask the daughter to step out so you can speak with the patient privately
C. Document the bruises and continue with the admission
D. Reassure the patient that everything will be confidential
‚úÖ Correct: B

Rationale: The priority is to SEPARATE the patient from the suspected abuser to allow private assessment. Remember SAFE: Separate first! Never confront the abuser (A), never promise confidentiality in abuse cases (D), and while documentation is important (C), safety assessment comes first.
Scenario 2: Respiratory Emergency
You are caring for a 68-year-old male with COPD who was given morphine 4mg IV for pain 30 minutes ago. You enter the room and find him difficult to arouse. His respiratory rate is 8 breaths/min, and his oxygen saturation is 85% on 2L nasal cannula. His skin is warm and dry.
What is your FIRST action?
A. Increase oxygen to 6L via nasal cannula
B. Document findings and notify provider
C. Stimulate the patient and prepare to administer Naloxone
D. Check ABG values
‚úÖ Correct: C

Rationale: This patient is showing signs of opioid-induced respiratory failure (OIRF) - RR of 8 is dangerously low! The priority is to reverse the opioid with Naloxone while supporting the airway. Don't increase O‚ÇÇ blindly in COPD (A), and don't delay treatment for documentation (B) or diagnostics (D). Remember: ABCs always come first!
Scenario 3: Therapeutic Communication
A 45-year-old patient with newly diagnosed diabetes tells you, "I don't understand why this is happening to me. I've tried to eat right. Why should I even bother learning all this? It won't make a difference anyway."
Which response demonstrates therapeutic communication?
A. "You shouldn't think that way. Diabetes is very manageable if you follow your treatment plan."
B. "It sounds like you're feeling overwhelmed and discouraged right now."
C. "Why do you think learning about diabetes won't help you?"
D. "Everything will be fine once you learn how to manage it."
‚úÖ Correct: B

Rationale: This reflects and validates the patient's feelings (V.E.R.B.S. - Validate & Reflect). Option A gives advice, C asks "why" (sounds judgmental), and D gives false reassurance. Remember: Emotion BEFORE education! Address feelings first, then teach.
Scenario 4: COPD Oxygen Management
You are caring for a patient with severe COPD who was admitted with shortness of breath. The current oxygen order is 2L via nasal cannula with a goal SpO‚ÇÇ of 88-92%. The patient's SpO‚ÇÇ is currently 85%. The patient's family member asks you to "turn up the oxygen so mom can breathe better."
What is the BEST nursing response?
A. Increase the oxygen to 4L and reassess in 15 minutes
B. Assess the patient, contact the provider, and explain that COPD patients require carefully titrated oxygen
C. Tell the family member that the current oxygen level is appropriate
D. Apply a non-rebreather mask at 15L
‚úÖ Correct: B

Rationale: While 85% is below the goal range of 88-92%, you must assess the patient first and get a provider order before changing oxygen levels in COPD patients. Too much oxygen can suppress their respiratory drive! Never increase O‚ÇÇ without assessment and orders (A, D), but also don't ignore that the patient is below goal range (C). The best response combines assessment, collaboration, and education.

‚úÖ NCLEX-Style Practice Quiz

1. A nurse is caring for a client who reports increased stress related to work deadlines. Which intervention should the nurse implement FIRST?
A. Administer prescribed anti-anxiety medication
B. Teach progressive muscle relaxation techniques
C. Reduce environmental stimuli and provide a quiet space
D. Encourage the client to discuss work problems in detail
Correct Answer: C
Rationale: Modify the ENVIRONMENT first before medications or teaching. Remember CALM - Control environment is step 1! Reducing stimuli provides immediate relief and is the least restrictive intervention.
2. A nurse suspects a client is experiencing abuse. Which action should the nurse take?
A. Promise the client that all information will remain confidential
B. Interview the client privately, away from potential abuser
C. Confront the suspected abuser about inconsistencies in the story
D. Wait for the client to voluntarily report the abuse
Correct Answer: B
Rationale: SAFE - Separate first! Never promise confidentiality in abuse cases (mandatory reporting), never confront the abuser, and never wait passively. The priority is private assessment.
3. A client receiving morphine for pain develops respiratory rate of 8 breaths/min. Which medication should the nurse prepare to administer?
A. Flumazenil
B. Naloxone
C. Epinephrine
D. Albuterol
Correct Answer: B
Rationale: Naloxone (Narcan) is the opioid reversal agent for opioid-induced respiratory failure. Flumazenil reverses benzodiazepines, epinephrine is for anaphylaxis/cardiac arrest, and albuterol is a bronchodilator.
4. Which statement by a client indicates understanding of therapeutic communication principles?
A. "The nurse asked me how I was feeling about my diagnosis."
B. "The nurse told me everything would be okay."
C. "The nurse asked me why I was feeling sad."
D. "The nurse gave me advice about what I should do."
Correct Answer: A
Rationale: Open-ended questions that explore feelings are therapeutic. False reassurance (B), asking "why" (C), and giving advice (D) are all non-therapeutic blocks to communication.
5. The nurse is reviewing ABG results: pH 7.32, PaCO‚ÇÇ 48, HCO‚ÇÉ 24, PaO‚ÇÇ 75. How should the nurse interpret these findings?
A. Respiratory acidosis
B. Metabolic acidosis

D. Metabolic alkalosis
Correct Answer: A
Rationale: pH is low (acidosis), PaCO‚ÇÇ is high (respiratory cause), HCO‚ÇÉ is normal. This indicates uncompensated respiratory acidosis. Remember normals: pH 7.35-7.45, PaCO‚ÇÇ 35-45, HCO‚ÇÉ 22-26.
6. A client with COPD has an oxygen saturation of 86% on room air. What is the appropriate oxygen goal for this client?
A. 95-100%
B. 92-96%
C. 88-92%
D. 80-85%
Correct Answer: C
Rationale: COPD patients typically have lower baseline oxygen levels and their goal SpO‚ÇÇ is usually 88-92% to avoid suppressing respiratory drive. Higher levels (A, B) can be dangerous in COPD.
7. Which finding indicates early respiratory distress in a client?
A. Cyanosis
B. Bradycardia
C. Restlessness
D. Hypotension
Correct Answer: C
Rationale: Restlessness is an EARLY sign of hypoxia. Cyanosis (A), bradycardia (B), and hypotension (D) are all LATE signs. Remember: Don't wait for cyanosis - it's too late!
8. A client in restraints must be monitored for which complications? (Select priority)
A. Impaired circulation and skin breakdown
B. Anxiety and anger
C. Nutritional deficits
D. Social isolation
Correct Answer: A
Rationale: CMS + ABCs! Monitor Circulation, Mental status, and Skin integrity as priority complications. While emotional effects (B, D) matter, physical safety is the priority.
9. Which respiratory medication provides the FASTEST relief for acute bronchospasm?
A. Albuterol (short-acting beta agonist)
B. Fluticasone (inhaled corticosteroid)
C. Montelukast (leukotriene modifier)
D. Theophylline
Correct Answer: A
Rationale: Albuterol is the RESCUE medication - it works fast! Corticosteroids (B) reduce inflammation but aren't immediate, montelukast (C) is preventive, and theophylline (D) has a narrow therapeutic range and isn't first-line for acute relief.
10. A client says "I'm so frustrated with my treatment. Nothing is working!" What is the nurse's BEST initial response?
A. "You should give the treatment more time to work."
B. "Why do you think it's not working?"
C. "It sounds like you're feeling really discouraged."
D. "Don't worry, I'm sure things will improve."
Correct Answer: C
Rationale: V.E.R.B.S. - Validate and Reflect the emotion! Option A gives advice, B asks "why" (judgmental), and D gives false reassurance. Always address EMOTION before EDUCATION.
